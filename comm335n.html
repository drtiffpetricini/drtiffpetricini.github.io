Welcome!

Hello! I’m Dr. Tiffany Petricini, and I’ll be your instructor for COMM 335N. This course explores the critical role of media in social justice and public humanities, providing you with hands-on experience in digital storytelling and community engagement. Here’s everything you need to get started and succeed!

Pronouns: she/her/hers | You’re welcome to call me Tiffany or Professor Petricini.

How This Course Works

COMM 335N is a structured yet flexible 16-week course designed to engage you in active learning, creative expression, and community involvement. Here’s a breakdown of what to expect and how to make the most of your experience:

  • Class Structure: We meet in person on Tuesdays and Thursdays from 1:35 PM to 2:50 PM in Lilley Library/Kochel 060. Each session will offer a mix of discussions, activities, and media exploration to deepen your understanding of public humanities and social justice.
  • Weekly Modules: Each week’s module on Canvas will include readings, assignments, and prompts to guide your learning. Modules open at the start of each week, so you’ll have time to look ahead and plan your study schedule.
  • Assignments: You’ll engage with a range of assignments, including:
    • Weekly Reading Preps that connect course materials to your personal reflections.
    • Hands-on Projects such as a podcast, documentary, or digital archive that allows you to explore a social justice issue in depth.
    • Peer Feedback and Group Work to foster collaboration and learning from others.
  • Grading: You’ll have control over your learning goals through our grading menu, where you can choose the level of engagement to achieve your desired grade. This approach emphasizes consistency and completion over traditional point-based grading.
  • My Role and Your Role: My goal is to support your learning journey by providing resources, feedback, and encouragement. I’m here to answer questions and help you succeed. Your role is to actively participate, complete assignments, and communicate openly about any challenges you encounter.
  • Flexibility and Support: I understand that life can be unpredictable. If you need an extension or extra help, please reach out to me as soon as possible so we can make adjustments to keep you on track.

By staying engaged with the readings, assignments, and class discussions, you’ll gain a well-rounded understanding of the role media can play in shaping narratives of social justice and public understanding. Let’s work together to make this a transformative and meaningful experience!

Grading Menu

Choose your final grade based on your engagement with course assignments:

Grade Requirements
A (100%) Complete all 8 assignments at “meets expectations” or higher, including all weekly reading preps and the final project.
B (80-89%) Complete at least 7 of the 8 assignments at “meets expectations” or higher, including the final project.
C (70-79%) Complete at least 6 of the 8 assignments at “meets expectations” or higher.
D (60-69%) Complete 5 of the 8 assignments at a basic or higher level.
F (below 60%) Complete fewer than 5 assignments or fall below the basic level on completed assignments.

Expectations Guide

Assignments will be evaluated based on the following standards:

  • Exceeds Expectations: Demonstrates a deep understanding and application of course concepts with creative or thorough engagement.
  • Meets Expectations: Shows good understanding of concepts and fulfills all assignment requirements.
  • Basic: Covers the assignment requirements but lacks depth or full engagement with the material.
  • Does Not Meet Expectations: Does not adequately address the assignment requirements or demonstrates minimal understanding.
