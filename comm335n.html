Welcome!

Hello! I’m Dr. Tiffany Petricini, and I’ll be your instructor for COMM 335N. This course explores the critical role of media in social justice and public humanities, providing you with hands-on experience in digital storytelling and community engagement. Here’s everything you need to get started and succeed!

Pronouns: she/her/hers | You’re welcome to call me Tiffany or Professor Petricini.

How This Course Works

COMM 335N is a structured yet flexible 16-week course designed to engage you in active learning, creative expression, and community involvement. Here’s a breakdown of what to expect and how to make the most of your experience:

  • Class Structure: We meet in person on Tuesdays and Thursdays from 1:35 PM to 2:50 PM in Lilley Library/Kochel 060.
  • Weekly Modules: Each week’s module on Canvas will include readings, assignments, and prompts to guide your learning.
  • Assignments: You’ll engage with a range of assignments, including:
    • Weekly Reading Preps that connect course materials to your personal reflections.
    • Hands-on Projects exploring social justice issues.
    • Peer Feedback and Group Work to foster collaboration.
  • Grading: Control your learning goals through our grading menu.
  • My Role and Your Role: My goal is to support your learning journey by providing resources, feedback, and encouragement. Your role is to actively participate, complete assignments, and communicate openly.
  • Flexibility and Support: If you need an extension or extra help, please reach out to me as soon as possible.

Grading Menu

Grade Requirements
A (100%) Complete all 8 assignments at “meets expectations” or higher, including at least 12 of 12 weekly reading preps and the final project.
B (80-89%) Complete at least 7 of the 8 assignments at “meets expectations” or higher, including at least 10 of 12 weekly reading preps and the final project.
C (70-79%) Complete at least 6 of the 8 assignments at “meets expectations” or higher, including at least 8 of 12 weekly reading preps.
D (60-69%) Complete 5 of the 8 assignments at a basic or higher level, including at least 7 of 12 weekly reading preps.
F (below 60%) Complete fewer than 5 assignments or fewer than 7 of 12 weekly reading preps, or fall below the basic level on completed assignments.

Expectations Guide

Exceeds Expectations: Demonstrates a deep understanding and application of course concepts with creative or thorough engagement.
Meets Expectations: Shows good understanding of concepts and fulfills all assignment requirements.
Basic: Covers the assignment requirements but lacks depth or full engagement with the material.
Does Not Meet Expectations: Does not adequately address the assignment requirements or demonstrates minimal understanding.

Collaborative Learning Pact

This course is built on mutual respect and collaboration. Together, we commit to the following:

  • Open Communication: We will be transparent in sharing ideas and feedback, always with respect.
  • Respect for Diversity: We acknowledge and embrace each other’s unique perspectives, backgrounds, and skills.
  • Active Participation: Each of us will contribute to discussions, group work, and activities to enrich our collective learning.
  • Supportive Environment: We will support one another, understanding that everyone learns at their own pace.
