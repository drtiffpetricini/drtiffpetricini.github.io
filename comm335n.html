Welcome!

Hello! I’m Dr. Tiffany Petricini, and I’ll be your instructor for COMM 335N. This course explores the critical role of media in social justice and public humanities, providing you with hands-on experience in digital storytelling and community engagement. Here’s everything you need to get started and succeed!

Pronouns: she/her/hers | You’re welcome to call me Tiffany or Professor Petricini.

How This Course Works

  • This is a 16-week online course starting January 13, 2024.
  • Asynchronous: Your learning is conducted at your own pace, but assignments are due weekly.
  • No textbook is required—all readings are provided in Canvas.
  • This course is not self-paced; you’ll participate in weekly discussions and activities with peers.
  • Weekly modules open every Monday, containing videos, readings, quizzes, and collaborative projects.

Grading Menu

Choose your final grade based on your engagement with course assignments:

Grade Requirements
A Complete all assignments at “meets expectations” or higher, including the final project.
B Complete at least 80% of assignments at “meets expectations” or higher, including the final project.
C Complete at least 70% of assignments at “meets expectations” or higher.
D Complete 60% of assignments with a basic or higher level.
F Less than 60% of assignments completed or below basic level.

Expectations Guide

Assignments will be evaluated based on the following standards:

  • Exceeds Expectations: Demonstrates a deep understanding and application of course concepts with creative or thorough engagement.
  • Meets Expectations: Shows good understanding of concepts and fulfills all assignment requirements.
  • Basic: Covers the assignment requirements but lacks depth or full engagement with the material.
  • Does Not Meet Expectations: Does not adequately address the assignment requirements or demonstrates minimal understanding.
