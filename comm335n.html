Welcome to COMM 335N

Media, Social Justice, and the Public Humanities

Spring 2025 - Penn State Erie

Welcome!

Hello! I’m Dr. Tiffany Petricini, and I’ll be your instructor for COMM 335N. This course explores the critical role of media in social justice and public humanities, providing you with hands-on experience in digital storytelling and community engagement. Here’s everything you need to get started and succeed!

Pronouns: she/her/hers | You’re welcome to call me Tiffany or Professor Petricini.

How This Course Works

  • This is a 16-week course running from January 13, 2025, to May 2, 2025.
  • Location: In-person at Lilley Library/Kochel 060
  • Meeting Times: Tuesdays and Thursdays, 1:35 PM - 2:50 PM
  • Required Text: The Routledge Companion to Public Humanities Scholarship by Fisher-Livne & May-Curry, 2024.

My Commitment to You

I’m dedicated to supporting your learning experience. Whether it’s through flexibility, encouragement, or guidance, my aim is to foster a safe and inspiring environment where every voice can be heard. Together, we’ll explore topics that matter deeply and connect them to real-world impact.

My Teaching Philosophy

I believe that every student has valuable perspectives to share, and that a diverse classroom enriches our learning. I aim to create a respectful and inclusive space where each of you can engage with course content in meaningful ways. Let’s connect what we learn to our lives and broader social contexts.

Our Pact

What You Can Expect from Me:

  • Respect, flexibility, and support for your learning needs.
  • A well-organized course with clear guidance and feedback.
  • Diverse assignments designed to meet various learning styles.
  • Encouragement and a safe environment for idea-sharing.
  • Transparency about mistakes; we all learn from them.

What I Expect from You:

  • Respect and empathy toward your peers and myself.
  • Active engagement and timely communication.
  • Effort to meet deadlines or communicate when adjustments are needed.
  • Patience with technology and openness to learning from errors.
  • Self-compassion and a growth mindset.

Equity Statement

This course is an inclusive space where diverse perspectives are welcomed and valued. We learn best when we listen to each other and create a brave environment for shared growth, where making mistakes is part of the journey.

Tips for Success

Here’s how to make the most of our course:

  • Plan Ahead: Preview each module early and use reminders for due dates.
  • Start on the Course Homepage: It’ll direct you to weekly modules and updates.
  • Use the Study Guide: This resource helps you focus on key content.
  • Submit Assignments Early: Aim for at least a day before the deadline.
  • Reach Out: Let me know if you need help or face challenges; I’m here to support you.
  • Utilize Canvas: Use the To-Do List and Calendar to stay organized.

How to Reach Me

I’m available to help with questions or concerns. Feel free to email me at tzr106@psu.edu. I aim to respond within 24 hours on weekdays and will also check messages on weekends. If you’d like a Zoom meeting, just ask!
