How This Course Works

  • This is a 16-week online course starting January 13, 2024.
  • Asynchronous: Your learning is conducted at your own pace, but assignments are due weekly.
  • No textbook is required—all readings are provided in Canvas.
  • This course is not self-paced; you’ll participate in weekly discussions and activities with peers.
  • Weekly modules open every Monday, containing videos, readings, quizzes, and collaborative projects.

My Commitment to You

This is a challenging time, and I am here to support you as we navigate learning together. I’m committed to providing flexibility, support, and a safe environment to succeed. I’ll guide, believe in, and encourage you.

My Teaching Philosophy

I believe everyone is capable of learning and that diversity enhances the classroom experience. I design my courses to allow all students to participate thoughtfully and reflectively, connecting course content to their own lives and experiences.

Our Pact

What You Can Expect from Me:

  • I will treat you with dignity and respect and be flexible to support your needs.
  • I will provide a clear and organized course that supports meaningful learning.
  • I will offer various assignments to meet diverse learning needs.
  • I will actively participate in your learning.
  • I will create a supportive, safe environment for idea sharing and discussion.
  • I won’t be perfect; I’ll make mistakes and learn from them.

What I Expect from You:

  • Treat me and your peers with respect and dignity.
  • Be an active participant and aim to meet due dates.
  • Maintain open communication with me about your needs.
  • Contact me if you encounter challenges with due dates.
  • Be patient with technology and expect occasional issues.
  • Give yourself grace; mistakes are part of learning.

Equity Statement

Everyone is welcome here, and I’m committed to creating a learning experience that values and includes everyone’s perspectives. This is a brave space where we learn from one another, share openly, and make mistakes along the way.

Week 1 Success Kit

Getting off to a good start in an online course is essential. Use this plan to set yourself up for success:

  • Tuesday: Log in to Canvas, view the introductory video, and start the Orientation module.
  • Wednesday: Complete the "Getting to Know You" survey and agree to our pact.
  • Friday: Participate in the "What I Value" VoiceThread assignment.
  • Monday: Submit the Orientation Quiz and "Pick Your Grade Bundle" assignment.

Success Tips

To make the most of this course, consider these tips:

  • Be proactive: Review each week’s module early and set reminders for due dates.
  • Start on the course homepage: Each week, it’ll guide you to the current module and announcements.
  • Use the study guide: It’ll help you focus on key points and succeed on quizzes.
  • Submit early: Aim to complete assignments at least a day before the due date.
  • Communicate: Reach out to me if you face challenges; I’m here to help!
  • Track assignments in Canvas: The To-Do List and Calendar will help you stay on schedule.

How to Get Your Questions Answered

If you need help, reach out through the Canvas inbox or email me at tzr106@psu.edu. I respond within 24 hours on weekdays and will also check messages over the weekend. I’m happy to set up a Zoom meeting if that’s helpful for you!
