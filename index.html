Course Description

This course introduces students to the ideas and skills that comprise "public humanities," with a focus on digital media tools. It aims to develop student understanding of how public humanities work can address present-day problems, fostering empathy within and building bridges between persons, groups, and communities. It offers hands-on training in three forms of digital media: documentary film, podcasting, and web publishing. Through honest and respectful engagement with a diverse range of ideas, perspectives, and experiences, students will develop creative and critical thinking and problem-solving skills by locating, analyzing and applying information in project formation and production.

Course Objectives

  • Critically analyze the role of media in shaping social justice narratives.
  • Engage with digital media tools to create content that contributes to public dialogue on social issues.
  • Explore the intersection of public humanities and social justice through hands-on projects such as podcasts, documentaries, and web publishing.
  • Evaluate the ethical implications of using media for advocacy and public engagement.
  • Understand the relationship between media ecology, social justice movements, and public humanities.
  • Incorporate generative AI tools to assist with project development, critically assessing their impact on creative outputs.

Grading Overview

In this course, you get to choose the grade you want to work toward by following a Grade Menu. At the beginning of the course, you’ll select your target grade (A, B, C, etc.), and each grade level has clear requirements that you’ll need to complete to achieve it. The menu shows you exactly what assignments and standards are needed for each grade, so you can plan your work accordingly. This system is designed to give you control over your learning and help you focus on meeting the standards for the grade you want. Just follow the guidelines in the Grade Menu, and as long as you meet the requirements, you’ll earn your chosen grade!
